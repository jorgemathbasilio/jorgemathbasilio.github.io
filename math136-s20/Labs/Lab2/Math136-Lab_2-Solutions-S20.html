Problem 1¶

Explain what each of the five variables are. Be sure to write your answer using a markdown cell.

The first variable "SEX" refers to a student's gender, male or female.

The second variable "WT.SEPT" refers to a student's weight in September (Fall semester, i.e. beginning of the academic year).

The third variable "WT.APRIL" refers to a student's weight in April (Spring semester, i.e. end of the academic year).

The fourth variable "BMI.SEPT" refers to a student's BMI, or Body-mass Index, in September (Fall semester, i.e. beginning of the academic year).

The fifth variable "BMI.APRIL" refers to a student's BMI, or Body-mass Index, in April (Spring semester, i.e. end of the academic year).

No, I do not believe in the "freshman 15" myth is accurate. If it was, we would explect to see the majority of the data values in the weights in April to be 15 pounds larger than in September. By comparing the medians, we see that the median weights of students in April went up to 66.00 lbs compared to 64.00 lbs. However, this is only a 2 pound increase. Even the largest weight of 97 pounds increased to 105 lbs, which is only a 8 pound increase.

By looking at the box plots, we see that the weights in April are only slightly larger than in September.

In conclusion, we the "freshman 15" myth doesn't apply--at least to this data set!
